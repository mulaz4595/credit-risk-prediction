Key Insights & Findings

Income Stability is Critical

Customers with steady employment history show 40% lower default rates. Banks should prioritize employment verification and stability metrics.

Credit Utilization Warning Signs

Customers using over 80% of their credit limit are 3x more likely to default. This is an early warning signal for risk management.

Age Factor

Customers aged 25-35 show highest default rates (28%) compared to other age groups. Additional screening may be needed for this demographic.

Geographic Patterns

Default rates vary by region, with some areas showing 35% higher risk. Location-based risk pricing could improve profitability.

Business Recommendations

Implement Risk-Based Pricing

Use model scores to adjust interest rates. High-risk customers (score >0.6) should pay 2-3% higher rates to offset default risk.

Early Warning System

Monitor existing customers monthly. Those whose risk score increases by 20+ points need immediate attention from collections team.

Approval Thresholds

Set automatic rejection for scores above 0.7 (saves review time). Scores 0.4-0.7 need manual review. Below 0.4 can be auto-approved.

Portfolio Monitoring

Target portfolio should have no more than 5% high-risk customers. Current model helps maintain this balance while maximizing approvals.
